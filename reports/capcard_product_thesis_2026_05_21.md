# CapCard Product Thesis - 2026-05-21

CapCard is an internal evidence-card marketplace for AI-generated or tool-generated capabilities. Each card summarizes what was attempted, what passed, what failed, what is bounded, what is not claimed, and what human review remains.

The smallest useful product is not a public marketplace. It is an internal list of validated cards with traceability, limitations, no-go boundaries, and a reviewer checklist.

CapCard is different from a dashboard because it does not merely display status. It enforces a disciplined evidence contract: source paths, false action fields, limitations, and no public claim without review.

The product promise is modest but valuable: help humans inspect capability evidence without accidental promotion, upload claims, certification claims, or proof claims.

CapCard must never claim certified safety, production controller status, public theorem/proof/open-problem results, PETAL verification without PETAL/API evidence, Hugging Face upload without a real upload, or CapCard certification.

Best current modes:

- Internal capability marketplace.
- Evidence review cockpit.
- Command Center internal status marketplace.

Weak or blocked modes:

- Training/curriculum catalog until Qwen warning rows are repaired.
- Public marketplace until public-review policy exists.
